BUSTO DI SHAKESPEARE

William Shakespeare, was a famous playwright who wrote “Romeo and Juliet”, “Hamlet” and many other plays. His bust is situated near the medieval gate in piazza Bra in Verona. It was made by Sergio Pasetto, a sculptor who was born in 1936 in Quinzano, near Verona. The bust of William Shakespeare was made in 2003, bought by the Club of Juliet and later donated to the city of Verona. In the half length portrait, Shakespeare is portrayed with a grave face, looking at the sky. Near the sculpture there is a marble disc on which some verses from “Romeo and Juliet”are inscribed. The bust is in a good state of conservation and it has a privileged location. In 2005, the city decided to pay homage to him with a bust in Corso Porta Nuova. Shakespeare was sculpted by Sergio Pasetto with a proud and determined look, but at the same time with great realism, respecting the fashion of his time. Next to the statue, on a marble slab you can read some verses from Romeo and Juliet, in the original language and in Italian translation.

Sant'Elena Church

The church, inserted inside the architectural complex of the Cathedral of Verona, has a gabled facade facing west, preceded by a loggia. In the upper part opens a large window and two small eyes that illuminate the interior of the church. The plan of the church is a single room, with the plenum space separated from the presbytery by a series of three steps. In the apse is the high altar, dominated by the sixteenth-century altarpiece by Felice Brusasorzi depicting the Madonna carrying the Child, with Saints Stephen, Zeno, George and Helen. Along the side walls of the hall there are two altars located in round arches; in the one on the right is the altarpiece depicting the Madonna and Child and Saints Giovanni Nepomuceno, Elena and Caterina by Pietro Antonio Rotari, which replaced a previous work by Antonio Badile; on the left the altar created by Giuseppe Antonio Schiavi, in which is the altarpiece depicting the Redeemer, Angels and Saints Francis of Sales and Philip Neri, work of John Peter Salvaterra.

Shakespeare's Biography

Shakespeare was born in Stratford-Upon-Avon (Warwickshire) 23rd of april, 1582 in a family of rich English merchants. He went to a local Stantfort school, where he studied Latin, rhetorics, logic and classic literature. In 1582 at the age of 18 years, he married Anne Hathaway, who was eight years older, with whom he had three sons. In 1592-1594 the theaters were closed due to a plague epidemic, and the artist could not work for the theater, so he devoted himself to poetry. After the reopening of the theaters, he began working for the most famous theater company of the time, which also performed at court, The Lord Chamberlain’s Men. Between 1594 and 1599, Shakespeare wrote some of his most extraordinary works: A Midsummer Night’s Dream (1595), Romeo and Juliet (1595). In 1599 William Shakespeare became one of the biggest shareholders and owner of the Globe Theatre of London. At the same time, The Lord Chamberlain’s Men became very popular and almost as known as the death of Queen Elisabeth I. In 1603, the new king Charles I Stuart adapted the company and changed the name to The King’s Men. William Shakespeare died 23 april 1616 in Stratford-upon-Avon, United Kingdom. Romeo and Juliet is one of William Shakespeare’s best-known tragedies and was composed between 1594 and 1596. The story, which follows the unfortunate love of two young people belonging to two rival families of Verona, the Montagues and the Capulets, has its roots in the classical tradition in the late Medieval (Masuccio Salernitano, Luigi da Porto). Dante Alighieri also quoted the two rival families in the sixth canto of Purgatory, making it a symbol of the political divisions of Italy at the time.

  • Romeo and Juliet
    The myth of Romeo and Juliet is celebrated in theatrical performances and revisitations during the whole year. The most famous event is the Shakespearean Festival which takes place every summer at the Roman Theatre, second in Europe after the one in Stratford on Avon. Among the tourist reception initiatives most appreciated by visitors Welcome to Verona. Romeo and Juliet a love story: the most beautiful scenes of the drama Shakespearean are recited during the summer, in Italian and English, in their ideal setting, the courtyard and Juliet's balcony, where legend has it that the two lovers really met and loved each other.
  • Nativity scenes and traditions
    The exhibition is held for the entire duration of the Christmas holidays International of Nativity scenes in Art and Tradition, set up in the arches of the Arena. The exhibition hosts around 400 specimens from the most important museums every year international and private collections. The installation is made with evocative special effects and offers a unique show, which attracts dozens of people every year thousands of visitors. Tocatì, International Street Games Festival During the Christmas period, Piazza Bra also hosts the Comet, a large one metal arch-sculptures by architect Rinaldo Olivieri, which emerge from the centre of the Arena, drawing a spectacular luminous arch seventy meters high. Designed for the inauguration of the first international exhibition of nativity scenes, the Comet Star is which has become the most famous symbol of Christmas in Verona. The Tocatì festival has also fully entered the Veronese traditions international street games, held at the end of September in the historic centre of the city: for three days the cars disappear to make room for dozens of games traditional, involving adults and children. Every year Tocatì welcomes a nation guest of honor, who presents his culture and traditions through spectacular performances.

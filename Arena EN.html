Portoni della Bra

Portoni della Bra is an access road located along the municipal walls of Verona, built to connect the city to what was then the sub-urban countryside. The doors are formed by two arched arches, concluded at the top by a dovetail battlement; between the two arches there is the clock installed in 1872. The building materials used were Veronese marble for the lower part and brick for the upper part, corresponding to the covered walkway and battlements. On the right of the door is the Pentagon tower, a battlemented defensive tower of the municipal era.

Piazza Bra

Piazza Bra, often shortened to Bra, is the largest piazza in Verona, Italy, with some claims that it is the largest in the country. The piazza is lined with numerous cafés and restaurants, along with several notable buildings. The Verona Arena, an amphitheater built nearly 2000 years ago, is now a world-famous music venue with regular operatic and contemporary music performances. Verona's town hall, the Palazzo Barbieri, also looks out across the piazza

Biografia di Dante

In 1306 he began to write his most important work "LA DIVINA COMMEDIA," which is, without doubt, the most representative work of Italian culture. It is an allegorical-didactic poem, a physical journey through hell and purgatory to paradise, but also a mental journey of purification. It is universally regarded as one of the greatest works of literature of all time, as well as one of the most important records of medieval civilization, so much so that it is known and studied all over the world.

Palazzo Cangrande

Palazzo Cangrande was commissioned by Cangrande I della Scala in the 14th century, that's where it takes its name. The building is also known as the Palace of the Podestà, Government, Province, or Prefecture due to its current function. It is situated between Piazza dei Signori, the Scaliger Arches, and Corso Santa Anastasia in Verona. It was the residence of the Scaligeri, the dynasty that ruled Verona for over a century, and became a true palace under Cangrande I della Scala. It was a center of power frequented by many illustrious men, including Dante, who stayed there in the early 14th century. After the fall of the Scaliger lordship, the palace became the seat of the podestà during the government of the Republic of Venice. In 1492 the building was expanded with Loggia del Consiglio in 1492. The final expansion, which gave the complex its definitive structure, was in 1560 with the construction of a long building mirroring the Cansignorio loggia. Today, the palace is owned by the Province of Verona and serves as the Prefecture's headquarters.

Palazzo Cansignorio

The tower was commissioned by Cansignorio della Scala towards the end of the thirteenth century, with the function of being a house-tower. It spreads on 5 floors, with a superior terrace which is closed by a merlon. Today the interiors of the tower still present some paintings from when the Scaligeri lived in the city. With the passing of time the structure was expanded until it got the aspect of a fortified court. In 1476 a loggia was added in the inner courtyard and between 1531 and 1533 a majestic gate created by Michele Sanmicheli was added to the facade.

BLOG POSTS

  • Romeo and Juliet
    The myth of Romeo and Juliet is celebrated in theatrical performances and revisitations during the whole year. The most famous event is the Shakespearean Festival which takes place every summer at the Roman Theatre, second in Europe after the one in Stratford on Avon. Among the tourist reception initiatives most appreciated by visitors Welcome to Verona. Romeo and Juliet a love story: the most beautiful scenes of the drama Shakespearean are recited during the summer, in Italian and English, in their ideal setting, the courtyard and Juliet's balcony, where legend has it that the two lovers really met and loved each other.
  • Nativity scenes and traditions
    The exhibition is held for the entire duration of the Christmas holidays International of Nativity scenes in Art and Tradition, set up in the arches of the Arena. The exhibition hosts around 400 specimens from the most important museums every year international and private collections. The installation is made with evocative special effects and offers a unique show, which attracts dozens of people every year thousands of visitors. Tocatì, International Street Games Festival During the Christmas period, Piazza Bra also hosts the Comet, a large one metal arch-sculptures by architect Rinaldo Olivieri, which emerge from the centre of the Arena, drawing a spectacular luminous arch seventy meters high. Designed for the inauguration of the first international exhibition of nativity scenes, the Comet Star is which has become the most famous symbol of Christmas in Verona. The Tocatì festival has also fully entered the Veronese traditions international street games, held at the end of September in the historic centre of the city: for three days the cars disappear to make room for dozens of games traditional, involving adults and children. Every year Tocatì welcomes a nation guest of honor, who presents his culture and traditions through spectacular performances.
